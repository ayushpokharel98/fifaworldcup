FIFA World Cup 2022

The FIFA World Cup, often simply called the World Cup, is an international association football competition contested by the senior men's national teams of the members of the Fédération Internationale de Football Association (FIFA, the International Federation of Association Football), the sport's global governing body. The championship has been awarded every four years since the inaugural tournament in 1930, except in 1942 and 1946 when it was not held because of the Second World War. The reigning champions are France, who won their second title at the 2018 tournament in Russia.
The format involves a qualification phase, which takes place over the preceding three years, to determine which teams qualify for the tournament phase. In the tournament phase, 32 teams compete for the title at venues within the host nation(s) over about a month. The host nation(s) automatically qualify.

Detailed Information about 2022 World Cup

This time world was held in Qatar. According to the football fans, this was the best worldcup ever because all of the matches were very interesting and unpredictable. Unexpected things happened like noone thought Morocco will progress so far and so on. Well, after 64 matches, a record 172 goals, one of the best (and most evenly priced) finals in history and the prior 12 years of existential footballing crisis, the Qatar 2022 World Cup finally reached its crescendo. In many ways, the footballing gods treated us to a fairytale like no other. Lionel Messi, the best to ever lace the cleats, lifted the one prize that always seemed to allude to him and extinguished any doubt that he sits alone in the pantheon of football greatness. Although you get the inkling, Kylian Mbappe will give his title a challenge in the coming years after pushing the 'little magician' all the way on the greatest stage.
The FINAL match was the most interesting one. It was Argentina vs France (Messi vs Mbappe). Argentina won it but France also played it well. Argentina led 2-0 at half-time through goals from Lionel Messi and Ángel Di María, but Kylian Mbappé equalised for France by scoring two goals in the space of two minutes in the second half. During extra time, Messi scored an additional goal and Mbappé scored a third goal which left the match tied 3-3. Argentina won the ensuing penalty shoot-out 4-2 to earn their third World Cup, and first since 1986.

Individual Awards in this World Cup

There are 4 types of Individual Awards in World Cup i.e. Golden Boot, Golden Ball, Best Young Player and Golen Gloves.
Golden Boot was taken by Kylian Mbappe for scoring 8 goals in this World Cup. Messi was behind him by scoring 7 goals.
Golen Ball was taken by Lionel Messi for showing the best performance in this World Cup.
Best Young Player Award was taken away by Enzo Fernandez.
Golden Gloves was taken by Emi Martinez for showing his amazing goal-keeping in this worldcup. He saved many crucial penalties. Richarlison's Goal against Serbia was voted the "Goal of the Tournament" by the football fans.
